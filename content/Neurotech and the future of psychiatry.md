When I was 16 years old, I made a threat on my life. It was much more of a cry for help than a legitimate attempt at death, but it got me admitted to an inpatient paediatric psychiatric facility. Upon admission I was asked many questions. Tell me about your childhood, your parents, your family, your friends. Tell me about school, and your teachers, and what you do when you are alone. These questions passed uneventfully. I had nothing of note to report. I was a normal teenage boy, who simply felt rot deep within his bones and wanted the pain to stop. The following morning I was promptly placed on the first line pharmacological treatment for depression in adolescents and begun daily therapy. It took me about 2 years, over 100 conversations with clinical psychologists and over 10,000mg of fluoxetine to drag myself into the light once more. I can't help but feel that the future of this illness is going to look very different.

I did not truly want to die; I knew even then that to be alive was far too much of a gift.
